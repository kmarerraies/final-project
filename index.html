Welcome to the AI era, where machines are getting smarter each day. In this world, AI isn't just a tool; it's transforming how we live and work. Picture self-driving cars, predictive phones, and AI-assisted doctors. It's not science fiction; it's our exciting reality. AI is changing jobs and creating new ones, from AI ethics experts to virtual reality designers. Join us on this journey through the AI landscape and its impact on the jobs of the future.

AI Ethicist/Algorithm Bias Auditor: As AI becomes more pervasive, there will be a growing need for professionals who can ensure that AI systems are developed and used ethically, without reinforcing bias or discrimination.
AI Trainer/Teacher: These individuals will train and supervise AI systems, helping them learn and adapt to new tasks, contexts, or data.
AI-driven Creativity Consultant: People who specialize in using AI tools and algorithms to enhance creative processes, whether in art, design, music, or writing.
Virtual Reality Experience Designer: VR, coupled with AI, will create immersive experiences. Designers will be needed to create these experiences for various industries, including gaming, education, and training.
AI Business Strategist: Individuals who can develop AI strategies for companies, helping them integrate AI technologies effectively into their operations.
Robot Behavior Specialist: Professionals who work on programming and designing the behavior of robots in various fields, from manufacturing to customer service.

the destiny of a few current jobs

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has the potential to automate various tasks and jobs across different industries.
here are some jobs and tasks that AI is likely to take over

Telemarketers: AI-powered chatbots can handle routine customer inquiries and marketing calls.
Pharmacists: Automated pharmacy systems can dispense medications.
Fast Food Cooks: Automated cooking and order fulfillment systems are being developed.
Basic Administrative Tasks: AI can automate administrative tasks like scheduling, appointment booking, and email sorting, reducing the workload of administrative assistants.
Basic Research and Data Retrieval: AI can assist in information retrieval, summarizing research papers, and generating basic reports, potentially impacting research assistant roles.
Inventory Management: AI can optimize inventory levels, order supplies, and predict demand, impacting inventory management roles.
